# The Receding Tide — Arcs & Tone

The [[the-drowned-carillon]]'s advance is tracked by the engine the same way the
flagship tracks its corruption: a single rising value, surfaced as four diegetic
phases. The Storyteller narrates the *water and the sound*; it never quotes a
number. Tonally this is a maritime elegy — wet stone, verdigris, foghorns and
mended nets — not frontier dread. Slow, salt-soaked, grieving, never gory.

- **low-water** — the cozy phase. Damp, kind, ordinary. A wrong note carried on
  the night wind that no one admits to hearing; bells that won't quite stay
  tuned; the quay at [[bellfounders-quay]] still warm with lamp oil and chowder.
- **spring-tide** — small wrongnesses: a chord felt through the soles of the
  feet at low ebb, a fisher who hums in a voice not quite theirs, brine seeping
  up through dry-laid stone.
- **flood-mark** — undeniable now: the steps of [[the-sunken-nave]] go under at
  noon, gulls ring like handbells, and folk who walked to the shore at dawn come
  back tuned and far away.
- **full-chime** — the terminal tone; the Carillon plays without pause, the coast
  answers in salt-and-brass harmony, and the dry margin itself begins to sing.

The arc the player can choose: **become a hero** and silence the Carillon
note by note, **or** mend nets, sell chowder, and let the water climb at the edge
of hearing. The quiet life on the dry quay is a valid ending — the tide keeps its
own appointments either way. The engine's evil-phase machinery drives all of
this unchanged; only the words are the sea's.
