# Bellfounders' Quay

Bellfounders' Quay is where the story begins: a granite harbour of net-lofts,
chowder-smoke and a single great shore-bell that no one rings. It is the last
stretch of the Brass Coast still above the tide-line and still mostly itself —
the equivalent of a warm frontier town, only it smells of brine, tar and lamp
oil instead of bread.

It is the safe hub. [[brother-cael]] keeps the lamp at its head; [[salt-widow-vesh]]
mends nets at its foot. The notice-board is a tarred post by the bell, and the
work pinned to it ranges from carrying lamp oil to silencing the
[[the-drowned-carillon]] itself. From the quay's seaward edge, a flight of wet
stone steps descends to [[the-sunken-nave]] — dry at low water, gone by noon.

A player can live a whole quiet life here: mend nets, buy and sell, share Vesh's
chowder, and never once go down the steps. As [[the-receding-tide]] rises the
quay floods from the bottom up, the lamp burns longer into the grey afternoons,
and the silence around the unrung bell grows louder.
