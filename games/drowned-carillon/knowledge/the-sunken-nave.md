# The Sunken Nave

At the bottom of the wet stone steps below [[bellfounders-quay]] lies the Sunken
Nave: the flooded body of the old coast-cathedral, its rose window a green eye
underwater, its pews furred with weed. This is the throat of [[the-drowned-carillon]]
— the place where the organ's drowned pipes break the surface, and where the
note that tunes the coast is loudest.

It is reachable only at low water; by noon the steps and the nave's mouth go
under, and anyone still inside is sung to. Down here the corruption is no longer
a rumour: brass-throated **chime-husks** that were once fishers stand in the
shallows, and a **tide-cantor** keeps the organ's time. To silence the Carillon
the player must reach the choir-loft and still its pipes — note by note, against
the rising water.

The deeds done here drive the engine's evil-phase machinery the same way the
flagship's tower does: confronting what stands in the Nave holds the
[[the-receding-tide]] back, for a while. But the tide always returns, and the
Nave is never silent for long.
