The premise

You wake where the
wheat turns wrong.

The player wakes in a forest clearing at the margin of Edgewood — a poor-but-proud frontier village — while an ambiguous Assistant watches. It is never a tutorial fairy. It begins as a barn cat and grows into something robed and certain.

The world narrates; it never instructs. Dread arrives in small concrete images, not adjectives: stillborn lambs with brass teeth, bread that rings when it cracks, clocks that tick hours that never were.

The four evil phases

A corruption that ramps, never announces.

The Clockwork Dark is not a demon you can banish — it is a metastasizing logic. The whole interface re-tints from one hidden phase, cozy to clockwork.

The interface

A traveler's journal crossed with a clockmaker's ledger.

Second person — narration addressed to “you”, quietly, like a journal someone else keeps about you.
No AI-narrator labels — no “Awareness 12%”, no chosen-one language. Hidden mechanics stay hidden until discovered in-world.
Diegetic chrome — a world clock header, a day · time · weather footer, iron rules, a brass edge on the Assistant's bubble.
Show the wrongness in objects — never announce it. Beauty and dread both arrive as small concrete things.
