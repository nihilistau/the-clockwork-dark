# The Harvest — Signs of the Clockwork Dark

The [[the-clockwork-dark]] does not arrive with armies. It *harvests*. Its spread
shows in once-only world signs — the Doom Clock's beats — that the Narrator
weaves in as slow dread:

- **Cog-harvesters** crawl the south wheatfield, low and brass, cutting the
  stalks into rows too straight for any wind. The first sign most folk dismiss.
- **The brass-toothed scarecrow** in the east field wakes: it turns its stitched
  grin to follow the road, then to follow *you*.
- **Clockwork-vines** breach the forest margin, threading the birches with cold
  filigree that ticks where sap should run.
- **The tunnels open.** Beneath the old stump a seam of worked stone yawns — the
  passages under Edgewood, unsealed, breathing oil and old time. The way inward.
- **The tower assembles** on the horizon, gear fitting to gear, patient and
  enormous; it was not there yesterday.
- **Consumed.** The last clock-stroke falls and Edgewood folds into mechanism —
  bread, hearth, and name alike.

These are not quests. They are *weather*. The player may chase them, push back
([[doom-arcs]] engagement), or bake bread and let them pass at the edge of
hearing — until there is no edge left.
