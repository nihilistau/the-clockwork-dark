# The Forge & Brann Holt

Off the square, past the bakery's warm smell, the **Forge** keeps a colder kind of
hearth. **Brann Holt** shoes horses, mends pots, and strikes each word as carefully
as each blow. Where [[maris-hearth|Maris]] is the village's warmth, Brann is its
edge — the other anchor of the ordinary life a traveler can choose ([[the-notice-board]]):
you can pass the whole story at an anvil and never once go to the tower.

## What he makes

The Forge is a **crafting station** ([[systems-catalog]]) — `craft_item` recipes
gated to `edgewood_forge`:

- **Mend a Blade** — a whetstone and a tired knife become a honed one.
- **Forge a Warded Blade** — the real work. Brass filings the corruption shed,
  worked into steel with a tinker's sympathy charm, make a blade that *bites
  clockwork* — honest anti-Dark gear that does not lie about what it is, the way
  Ilya's charms sometimes do ([[the-tinkers]], [[sympathy-and-naming]]).
- **Reforge a Relic** — an old clock-part and brass filings, struck into a keepsake.

## The crystal he won't explain

Since the wheat turned, Brann forges fewer ploughshares and more blades, and works
wards he says he learned from no one. On the cold corner of his anvil sits a
**crystal that catches a light not in the room** — it hums, faintly, when the Dark
is near. He does not say where it came from. Some in the village think the quiet
friend who teaches the gear-child ([[the-two-powers]]) has been to the smithy too.
Brann only shrugs, and strikes the iron, and will not be drawn. The fire is honest
work; the rest he keeps to himself.
