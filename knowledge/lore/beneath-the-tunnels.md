# Beneath the Tunnels

One of the once-only signs the Dark crosses (the Doom Clock's `tunnels_open` beat,
see [[the-harvest]]) is a seam of worked stone yawning open beneath the old stump
outside Edgewood. The villagers call them *the tunnels* as if they were always
known. They were not. They were **sealed**, and the sealing is older than the
village.

What lies beneath:

- **The old passages.** Cut by hands that knew [[the-clockwork-dark]] by an
  earlier name — the same hands that raised the Mage-Ruins above. They run inward,
  toward the [[the-heartlands]], straighter than any honest tunnel should, breathing
  a draught that smells of oil and old time.
- **The First Warden's barrow.** Under Hollow Hill the First Warden sleeps, and
  *not alone*. The barrow was a lid, not a tomb: something was put down here and
  weighted with a saint nobody can name. The shrine-keeper Greta will not finish
  the mural for the same reason the old folk will not say the saints' names — to
  name a thing is to let the Dark model it ([[sympathy-and-naming]]).
- **The inward road.** The tunnels are not a dungeon to loot. They are the
  Clockwork Dark's *commute* — the route by which the Heartlands' logic reaches
  the margin without crossing the honest miles above. When they open, the clock
  has found a shortcut.

To go down is to trade the slow margin for the fast center. A traveler may seal a
seam (engagement against the Dark; see [[doom-arcs]]) or follow it inward toward
[[the-convergence]]. Either way, the tunnels are the moment the quiet life learns
it was always sitting on a lid.
