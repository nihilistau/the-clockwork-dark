# The Clockwork Dark

The Clockwork Dark is the slow wrongness creeping out of the Heartlands. It does
not burn or storm; it *replaces*. Wheat stands in rows too straight for wind.
Livestock grow brass behind the teeth. A name spoken too often in the deep woods
answers back in a voice of turning gears. It advances whether or not anyone is a
hero — the world ticks toward mechanism on its own.

Its hunger is for **names and intention**: the more a thing is known and willed,
the more the Dark can model and unmake it. This is why [[sympathy-and-naming]] are
both the player's sharpest tools and their greatest risk. It spreads out of
[[the-heartlands]], the country at the center of the wound.

The corruption is *organic-mechanical*, never steampunk spectacle — gears in
bone, filigree in frost, a clock's patience rather than a dragon's roar. Its
spread is measured in [[evil-phases]]; the village of Edgewood sits at the
margin, still mostly untouched, where folk like [[maris-hearth]] bake bread and
pretend the roads haven't started to change.
