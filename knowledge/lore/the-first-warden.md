# The First Warden & the Older Name

Once the tunnels open the hidden path ([[the-tunnel-mouth]], [[beneath-the-tunnels]]),
it climbs past the worked stone to **Hollow Hill** — a turf-roofed barrow crowned
with leaning stones — and the broken **Mage-Ruins** beyond it. Both have waited a
very long time for someone to come asking the right question.

## The First Warden

The barrow holds the **First Warden**, who held the line when the Clockwork Dark
first stirred, ages before Edgewood. The Dark reached them in the end as it
reaches everything: gears turn slow where the heart was, brass threads the old
bones, and still — out of habit older than death — the husk keeps its vigil on
the stone seat. It is not an enemy. Raise a hand against it and it defends itself
even now, faster than grief; some things the Dark has *finished* are warnings, not
foes. The lesson of the barrow is that force is the wrong tool here.

## The older name

The mages who raised the ruins did not fight the Dark with iron. They **bound it
with its true name** — the *older name* — and cut that name into a great solar
star-chart, now broken across the ruined hall's floor. To take the name you must
read the standing stones (they name the binding-star plainly: the **Hollow Crown**)
and then name the right constellation on the chart.

A **faceless figure in lichened robes** waits by the chart, one violet-lit hand
half-raised to point — the Assistant in a stranger, higher form ([[the-two-powers]]),
fond of you the way a god is fond of a candle, and **not to be trusted on the
naming**: its pointing is not the same as its meaning. A player who read the stones
holds the name and does not need its help; one who didn't must gamble against a
liar. Naming is sympathy turned to a key ([[sympathy-and-naming]]).

## Why it matters at the end

Learn the older name and `knows_older_name` is set. At the **convergence reckoning**
([[the-convergence]]), the *unmake* choice — *turning the Dark's own naming back on
it* — is sharpened by exactly this: a true name cuts both ways. The barrow is where
a player earns the edge that makes refusal at the tower more than a prayer.
