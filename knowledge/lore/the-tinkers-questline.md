# The Tinker's Reckoning — Ilya's Questline

A short, opt-in arc offered by **Ilya of the Nine Pins** ([[the-tinkers]]) — who is,
or may be, the Assistant wearing a tinker's face ([[the-two-powers]]). Like
everything on [[the-notice-board]], it is a thread the player may pull or leave; the
Storyteller narrates the work and the engine grants the reward on genuine
completion. The steps live in `data/contracts.yaml` and gate by awareness / evil
phase so they unlock in rough order as the player notices more.

The chain:

1. **Ilya's Errand: Brass Filings** — gather the curls the corruption sheds where it
   has passed. "For a lamp I am building. You'll see." (Coin, filings, goodwill.)
2. **Ilya's Question: Read the Road** — walk the Marches and bring back what the
   milestones *say* — not the distance, the other number. "I need to know how fast it
   is counting." (Coin, a little awareness, a tinker's map.)
3. **Ilya's Work: The Naming Lamp** — light a sympathy lamp at a corrupted margin with
   a name spoken into its reservoir. "Sympathy cuts both ways. We will teach the light
   to say *no*." An `anti_dark` step: it raises **engagement** and yields a real tool,
   the sympathy lamp ([[sympathy-and-naming]]).
4. **Ilya's Confidence: Nine Pins** — with the lamp lit, Ilya tells you a little truth,
   or a little lie: "I am not always from here. I have other roads, other faces. But I
   am fond of this small place, the way one is fond of a single candle." A ward is
   pressed into your hand; the only price is to *hold the line a while longer*.

The questline is deliberately ambiguous about whether Ilya is a person or the
Assistant — the help is real, the trust is not owed, and the capstone asks for
engagement rather than giving an answer. It is the [[doom-arcs]] engagement lever
dressed as a relationship: a way to be pulled, gently, toward pushing back on
[[the-clockwork-dark]] without ever being commanded to. See [[the-convergence]] for
where holding the line eventually leads.
