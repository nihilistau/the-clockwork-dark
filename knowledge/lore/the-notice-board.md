# The Notice Board & Contracts

In Edgewood square a board of weathered notices hangs by the well, and the folk
themselves have small needs. This is how the player chooses their own weight in
the world — nothing is forced.

A traveler may take **nothing**: bake at Maris's oven ([[maris-hearth]]), carry
loaves, learn the craft, and let the [[doom-arcs]] clock run at the edge of
hearing until — eventually — it doesn't. That ending is valid.

Or they may pull a notice down. Contracts come in three tempers:
- **mundane** — the dawn-bread delivery, the tinker's lost cog. Coin and goodwill;
  the quiet life with a little structure.
- **bounty** — the brass scarecrow that no longer stands where it was put. Risk
  for coin and a small blow against the Dark.
- **anti_dark** — still the south harvester before the field is lost. These raise
  **engagement** and hold the clock back ([[doom-arcs]]).

The work itself is narrated by the Storyteller and resolved by the engine —
often as combat or an [[ephemeral-challenges]] challenge — and the **reward is
engine-granted** on completion (coin, items, reputation, engagement). Tinkers and
the Assistant-as-tinker often have the strangest postings. Take one, or don't;
the Clockwork Dark keeps its own appointments either way.
