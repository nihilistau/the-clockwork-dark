# The Heartlands

The Heartlands are the old center of the country — once its breadbasket and its
capital of small certainties, now the place the roads count *toward*. Edgewood
sits at the frontier margin; the Marches run inward from it; and somewhere past
the last honest milestone the Heartlands begin, where the [[the-clockwork-dark]]
first learned to keep time.

Nobody who walks in walks back the same, and few walk back at all. The refugees
at [[the-notice-board]]'s edge and the militia holding Millhaven gate are the
runoff of a country that is being *finished* — not burned, not conquered, but
**replaced**, field by field and name by name, on the patient clock of the
[[evil-phases]]. The wheat there does not blow. It ticks.

What the Heartlands were is hard to recover, because the Dark unmakes the memory
along with the thing: ask three old folk where the capital stood and you will get
three roads, each a little wrong. This is not propaganda. It is **harvest**
(see [[the-harvest]]) reaching backward through what is known. The further inward
you go, the more the country agrees with the clock about what it always was.

The Heartlands are why the quiet life is a real choice and a doomed one at once:
Edgewood can bake bread a long time at the margin, but the margin is where the
Heartlands end *today*. Tomorrow's edge is a little nearer the oven. See
[[doom-arcs]] for the pace, and [[the-convergence]] for where the counting stops.
