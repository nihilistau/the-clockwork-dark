# Sympathy & Naming

The only craft that bites [[the-clockwork-dark]] back is also the craft the Dark
feeds on. This is the central irony of the world, and it is mechanical, not just
thematic.

## Sympathy — the unmaking
To work *sympathy* is to know a thing so precisely that you can speak its making
and loosen it. Against clockwork foes this is the **unmaking**: the engine resolves
it as a focus-fuelled check, and on a hit it does true damage to anything tagged
`clockwork`, where steel only chips it (see the combat engine, [[the-harvest]]).
A sympathy lamp, a ward bell, a name spoken into a reservoir at a corrupted margin
— tinker work ([[the-tinkers]]) — are sympathy made portable.

## Naming — the double edge
But the Dark's hunger is *for names and intention*. The more a thing is known and
willed, the more completely the clockwork logic can model it — and model is the
first step of replace. So every act of sympathy is a small risk: you light a lamp
against the Dark and, for an instant, the Dark sees the shape of you lighting it.

This is why:
- the saints of Edgewood's shrine have **no names**, and Greta will not finish the
  mural — an unnamed thing is harder to harvest;
- the old folk will not say what sleeps [[beneath-the-tunnels]];
- the Assistant ([[the-two-powers]]) gives its sharpest help *unreliably* — to lean
  on a name too hard is to hand the clock a handle.

## The reckoning
At the end, in [[the-convergence]], the craft turns on itself one last time: the
player's last engine-resolved choice can be to **name the tower's making** and
refuse to be a part it can use — to be so fully *given, willed, and known already*
that the mechanism finds no shape left to model. Sympathy that cut both ways, made
to cut one way, once. See [[doom-arcs]] for how it all paces.
