# The Village Empties

The Clockwork Dark does not announce itself with armies. It announces itself by
the woodcutter who will not go to the trees, the shrine-keeper who stops tending
the candles, the road behind the gate growing thick with people going only one
way. As the Doom Clock crosses its beats ([[the-reactive-world]], [[doom-arcs]]),
named villagers **move** — and `npcs_at` shows it, so the Storyteller narrates a
village that is visibly pulling inward.

- **The vines breach the forest** → **Aldric Thorn**, the woodcutter, abandons the
  margin (`forest_forage` → `edgewood_square`). His axe stays idle against a wall;
  he will not work past the first birch ([[the-clockwork-dark]]).
- **The tunnels open** → **Greta Moss**, the shrine-keeper, leaves the candles
  ([[beneath-the-tunnels]]) and stands at the square's edge, watching the ground
  where the seam yawned.
- **The tower assembles** → the road behind **Millhaven gate** thins to refugees
  (`gate_thinning`); Sergeant Sera holds the line with fewer each day, and does
  not leave it ([[the-two-powers]]).

A displaced villager is marked (`displaced`) and wears a road-worn face. **Maris
the baker does not move** — she keeps her oven and her humming to the last, the
quiet life carried as far as it will go ([[maris-hearth]]). The map of who has
fled is itself a clock-face: read where people are standing and you can tell the
hour the Dark has reached.
