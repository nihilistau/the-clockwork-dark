# The Four Evil Phases

The [[the-clockwork-dark]]'s advance is tracked by the engine as `evil_progress`
(0.0–1.0), surfacing as four diegetic phases. The Storyteller narrates the
*signs*; it never quotes the number, and below the awareness threshold the worst
words are gated.

- **dormant** (0.0–0.2) — cozy frontier life. Bad dreams, a cold draft in a warm
  room, a rumor no one repeats twice.
- **stirring** (0.2–0.5) — small wrongnesses: brass glints in a scarecrow's grin,
  a road that takes an hour too long though the sun says otherwise.
- **spreading** (0.5–0.8) — the corruption is undeniable to the watchful:
  refugees, sickly chartreuse light, wheat standing in unnatural rows.
- **consuming** (0.8–1.0) — stark, high-contrast horror; the Heartlands hunt
  names and the horizon itself is coming apart.

Phases gate content (cutscene milestones, the Marches arc) and re-tint the whole
interface. Crucially, the baker who never leaves Edgewood is *allowed* to stay in
dormant for a long time — the player's quiet life is valid.
